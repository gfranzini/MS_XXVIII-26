About

Digital Edition

This digital edition was built upon the information gathered and published in the ongoing Catalogue of Digital Editions. The purpose of the Catalogue is to survey the evolving field of digital (scholarly) editing by collecting relevant projects and analysing their features. In this way, it is possible to visualise trends, including which historical periods or languages are most covered, how many projects use which types of licenses, or which countries appear to be more active in the production of digital editions.

The digital edition of MS XXVIII(26) has undergone three redesigns. The version you see here is the third and final design, which makes no use of an underlying database to organise content. What you see is an HTML website with a jQuery script for image-zooming and Bootstrap for a responsive front-end.

Manuscript

Below is a brief codicological assessment of MS XXVIII(26):

  • Early 5th century
  • Mixed parchment quality
  • No decorations/illuminations
  • Many marginalia
  • Restored by the Biblioteca Vaticana between 1923-1927
